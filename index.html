The Mysterious Disappearance of Rhapsody in Metropolis

The film itself, similar to Metropolis, was to be set in the future around the 3000's in terms of years, more than 80 decades later. The film Rhapsody in Metropolis was supposed to be released in theaters 2005, but all of a sudden it was taken off the roster of films being released in June 2005, and was never heard from again. Along with the supposed filmmakers who were directing it were unheard from. Trying to reach out to actors only to find out they were not professional actors, but rather everyday people who have auditioned for their roles, like what film director Abbas Kiarostami had done for his films "Where is Friend's house" and "Life, and Nothing More..."

When we had looked for said actors, we also found they were from multiple parts of the world such as Eastern Europe, Central America, North America, South Asia, Australia, North Africa, and East Asia. Some of the actors were impossible to find, considering when we did this project, most of them are much older now with families and jobs, however we did find one of the main protagonists who served as what the poster makes her look like some sort or celebrity or idol of the upper class. We set up an interview with her September 27, 2018, and she wanted her identity to remain anonymous. The interview went smoothly despite her constant wants of her children, but we could see why she fit perfectly in her role for the film with her strength and demeanor, handling everything with grace and poise, composing herself as she told her children to wait in their rooms as we interviewed her.

"Ah 'Rhapsody in Metropolis,'" she spoke as she sipped a cup of water. She had recalled working with everyone, auditioning in it as a teenager and being given the role as the idol. She had said her character was a revoluitonaire, a fighter in a sense who came from the bottom to the top, now wanting to help those who are at the bottom as well. When we had asked about the film's disappearance, she had frowned and responded delicately. "The film never really disappeared, it is accessible but you will have to find someone who owns the films, in any case it would be the actors or the rest of the people who worked on the film. Some decided not to keep the film and I know one person who decided to keep it as a memorial, digging it in his backyard as to keep it hidden, like a time capsule of sorts. When we were told that the film wouldn't be in theaters, the actors were shocked, only the people who worked behind the screen seemed upset since film work was their career, unlike the actors who had seperate jobs and lives. The directors had told us that the world wasn't ready to experience this film yet, it would cause distress to most, so everyone reluctantly agreed and were gifted VHS tapes of the completed film."
